## The Story

Yono Kazeshima was nineteen years old, worked part-time at a convenience store, and spent most of his evenings talking to a cream bun he had been saving money for since Tuesday.

He died on a Tuesday.

Not heroically. Not tragically. He slipped off a pedestrian bridge in Tokyo trying to rescue a cat that did not want to be rescued, landed on a delivery truck full of blank-faced mannequins, and had one final thought:

> *"I never ate the cream bun."*

He woke up in a white room. Standing across from him was a woman with violet hair, red eyes, a very short skirt, and the expression of someone who had just accidentally broken something expensive.

Her name was Violet Arden. She was a goddess.

She had pushed him.

She called it an incident. He called it dying. She offered compensation. He picked time control and regeneration because he had read enough stories to know what survived. His soul screamed for a tennis racket that fired rockets, and he got one, because the universe apparently thought that was funny.

Then something older than the white room knocked on its walls.

And Violet's expression went cold for the first time.

**Yono Kazeshima was reborn into Asteria.**

He did not arrive as a hero. He arrived confused, slightly concussed from the death, and immediately had to fight a smoke wolf in a city that was on fire.

## The World

**Asteria** is a world built on Threads — invisible connections between people, places, and promises. Every bond creates a Thread. Every broken oath leaves a scar in the fabric of reality. The oldest scars are sealed behind doors that should never open.

One of those doors has started to move.

The **Covenant Door** is not a place. It is a boundary. It separates the world from what existed before the world had rules. Something has been pressing against it from the other side for longer than Asteria has had cities. It does not have a name anyone alive can pronounce without losing part of themselves in the attempt.

Yono's palm carries a crescent mark that appeared the moment something noticed him in the white room.

Violet will not explain what it means. Not yet.

## Volume 1 — Reborn With Zero Dignity

*The Shade Debt Arc*

Yono falls into **Lumera**, a city under siege. The Warden — a sealed entity that feeds on broken promises — has broken through a gate. People are burning. A child named **Liri** is hiding in the wrong building.

Yono runs toward the first scream.

That is the rule Violet gave him. He does not know yet that it is the reason she chose him specifically. He does not know yet that the crescent on his palm is not a mark. It is a seal. And the thing it is sealing is not a monster from outside.

It is a version of himself.

Volume 1 covers his first week in Asteria. By the end of it, he has made three friends, destroyed one forest, and become something the oldest creatures in the world have started calling by a name he has not heard yet.

## Volume 2 — The Covenant Door

*The Door Arc*

The seal breaks.

Not completely. Not all at once. But enough. Enough that the thing on the other side of the Covenant Door stops pressing and starts listening.

Violet becomes less careful about explaining things. The party expands. The stakes stop being local and start being cosmological. Yono gets stronger every chapter — not because he trains or levels up, but because the seal is weakening and what it is holding back is not a power.

It is a decision he made before he was Yono.

> *The current Yono is always the strongest Yono.*

The limit that keeps him human is that he still needs someone to see him. If no one is looking, if no one is thinking about him, he goes quiet. Violet understands this before he does. It is why she stays close. It is not just strategy.

## The Characters

### Yono Kazeshima — 風嶋ヨノ
The protagonist. Died for a cat. Has been running toward screams ever since. He narrates everything like it is the opening of an epic and lives it like he is making it up as he goes. The gap between how he talks about himself and what he actually is grows wider with every chapter. He is aware of the gap. He finds it funny most of the time. Sometimes he does not.

### Violet Arden
The goddess who pushed him. She is precise, composed, and carries something very old behind her red eyes. She gives him rules instead of explanations. She stays closer than the rules require. She says this is strategy. She has said it enough times that she has almost convinced herself.

### Liri
The child from the burning city. She decided Yono was reliable approximately three minutes after he nearly got her killed. Her confidence in this has not wavered since. She is correct.

### Meryn
A healer who has kept everyone alive longer than anyone should need a healer to. She heals like every bandage is an argument with the past. The story of why is in the pauses between what she says and what she almost says.

### Mirika
She reads during fights. She has read during every fight. The book is always relevant. This is not a coincidence.

### Lyra
Strikes first, asks later, never asks later because the answer was already in the strike. She and Yono have a working relationship based on mutual acknowledgement that neither of them thinks before moving.

### Velkor
He is sealed beneath the world. He calls Yono *little crown*. He has been patient for longer than Lumera has existed. He is not the villain. He is the reason there is a story. There is a difference.

## Themes

Threadborn is a story about what you choose to be when no one is forcing you to be anything.

Yono did not become small because he was hiding. He became small because he decided that a life that fit inside one day, with problems he could actually hold, and a cream bun at the end of the week, was a life worth having. The world he woke into is the opposite of that. It is vast and ancient and full of things that want him to be vast and ancient with them.

He keeps trying to go home. There is no home left. So he builds something in the space where home used to be, out of people he keeps running toward when they scream.

That is the story.

## EX Novel — The Chronicle

The EX Novel runs alongside the main volumes as a lore archive, exploring the power systems, character backstories, the Thread mechanics, the Velkor question, and the IF routes — alternate timelines showing what happens when Yono makes different choices at key moments.

The IF routes are not hypotheticals. They happened. The reason only one of them is the main story is something the EX Novel takes its time explaining.
